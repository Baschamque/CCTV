CCTV (Climate Change Tests & Visualizations) system is developed by SSEL (Smart & Sustainable Environment Laboratory) at KAIST for intuitively visualizing various quantifiable impacts of carbon emissive products and activities in global community and to facilitate public awareness enhancement as well as climate change associated decision makings.

The following steps are brief use case of this system.

  1. Navigating presets, ranging from baseline, moderate, and extreme.
  2. Choosing carbon-emitting activities, including the top 20 critical human activities that influence climate change ranging from electricity, road transportation, deforestation, etc., categorized into six main types: energy, transport, industry, agriculture, and building, and miscellaneous. Multiple carbon-emitting activities can be selected all at once.
  3. Choosing global-scale phenomenon and natural disasters that is affected by climate change, ranging from water scarcity, food insecurity, and extreme heatwaves, etc., categorized into six main types: water, heat, disasters, social, economic, and ecosystem.
  4. Adjusting the activity intensity: this controls the intensity level of the selected activity. This amplifies the metric being visualized when hypothesizing how these activities could worsen their various phenomenon if this activity is intensified in the real world.
  5. Adjusting the time horizon: this controls when the effect or phenomenon is evaluated, which highlights the sensitivity of certain activities and phenomenon. Users can assess the immediate to near-term consequences and also longer-term outcomes.
  6. Selecting multiple view modes: the current version of this interactive map shows several ways to visualize the severity or intensity of the phenomenon. There are 8 types of view modes, but there are mainly 6 different view mode (there are 3 duplicate visualizations, similar in concept but different libraries are used to visualized).
    These include: a. Bubbles/points b. Countries c. Heat d. Spikes/Hexbin/3D Heat e. Arcs f. 3D Countries

📋 Method & Assumptions

  • Impact Calculation: Based on activity CO₂ emissions, population factors, and time horizon projections
  • Random Factors: Each country has a cached random multiplier (0.7-1.3) for realistic variation
  • Time Scaling: Impact increases linearly with years from baseline (2024)
  • Population Factor: Uses logarithmic scaling to account for population size
  • Color Mapping: Values are categorized into 5 ranges based on impact type thresholds
  • Data Source: Country boundaries from Natural Earth, impact data calculated from embedded GeoJSON
